VELVETHEAD

VELVETHEAD features two strangers, Spit and Buck, who happen to experience euphoria in the middle of the night: a vision of the Velvethead. Velvethead is a celestial figure covered in jewelery, with skin made of red velveteen. Desperate to feel euphoria again, the duo lose their grip as they descend into grander and riskier attempts to get Velvethead's attention.

VELVETHEAD is a queer absurdist drama, told by a queer team, that probes the extremes individuals go to in order to recapture wonder and intimacy. This piece was of interest due to vivid, poetic writing of Dean Vukovic, which created a playground for design and composition. The play opens with a song, performed by a lone guitarist, who fades into the orchestra pit corner (alongside a synth player) to accompany and provide live sound effects for the show, using only their respective instruments.

Music is crucial to this brutal world. Music has the ability to change how performers perform, and how they're received. The score was built in the rehearsal room with a lyre, and evolved every night, reacting to the energy of the performers and the audience.
